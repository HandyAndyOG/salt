A short engaging pitch of yourself and why you’re applying

I came across </salt> and I immediately knew that this was the right path for me. Working in teams, mob programming, eight hours of practical experience each day and a breakdown of all the necessary tools to be successful in the industry is what drew me into . I am a self-taught junior full-stack developer with 1.5 years of experience. However, I require a deeper dive into JavaScript and its frameworks. I would love the opportunity of working in teams of like-minded people, practicing: pitching products, documenting reports of code, UX design days, logging, and unit testing. These are all skills that I have not been able to acquire in my current role.

Describe when your passion for coding started and why

At a young age of 19 I first encountered Computer Science as one of my modules and absolutely loved it, unfortunately my chosen degree went down another path. In my later years, I found myself unhappy with the state of the companies’ website. I tasked myself to fix bugs and make the website more attractive through WordPress. I soon came to realize that trying to fix bugs on the backend of WordPress very limiting and frustrating. I decided to spark an old love of mine and learn how to code a new website from scratch. Throughout the steep learning curves, frustrations, and many hours of research; I found an immense amount of joy and satisfaction. Like as if a spark in my mind was ignited, I was hooked to coding, breaking down large problems into smaller tasks and solving them. The days of not enjoying work, had ended and I felt a new chapter in my life starting. Since building the website, I have in my own spare time enrolled in the Odin-Project and the Python Institute to further expand my knowledgebase of JavaScript, NodeJS and Python.

Why Salt?

After listening to Marcus’s launch event, I am confident that the above structure is a good fit for me; I thrive in a high intensity work environment, where we are expected to figure out the problems at hand. By giving us faulty code or implementing mob programming, we are forced to understand the intricacies, thus building a solid coding-knowledge foundation. Additionally, the thought of working in a professional team and being placed in work environments provides unmatched learning potential.
